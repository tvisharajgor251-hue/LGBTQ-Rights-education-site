What Are SDGs?

The Sustainable Development Goals are a set of 17 global goals created by the United Nations to help improve the world by 2030.

SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities

Key aspects of SDG 10

  • Income inequality: Targets include ensuring the incomes of the bottom 40% of the population grow faster than the national average.
  • Discrimination: It calls for eliminating discriminatory laws, policies, and practices based on factors like gender, disability, race, or religion.
  • Migration: Aims to facilitate orderly, safe, and regular migration through planned policies and to reduce remittance costs.
  • Political and economic inclusion: Seeks to increase the political and economic representation of all people, especially marginalized groups.
  • Social protection: Encourages the adoption and implementation of social protection policies to support vulnerable populations.
  • International cooperation: Addresses inequality between countries through fair representation and development assistance.

Why SDGs Matter

SDGs help guide countries toward a better, safer, and more sustainable future. They focus on reducing poverty, protecting the planet, and improving the lives of people everywhere.
